Excerpt from Cicero's "On the Extremes of Good and Evil"

ENGLISH: Nor is there anyone who loves or pursues or desires to obtain pain of itself, because it is pain, but occasionally circumstances occur in which toil and pain can procure him some great pleasure.


LATIN: Neque porro quisquam est, qui dolorem ipsum quia dolor sit amet, consectetur, adipisci velit, sed quia non numquam eius modi tempora incidunt ut labore et dolore magnam aliquam quaerat voluptatem.

Analysis

This short excerpt from Cicero is a philosophical examination of why people approach and endure pain; they have a goal in mind. It’s quite a wise observation for something that would go on to become meaningless sample text on computers several thousand years in the future. The actual reason why the original Latin text was jumbled into nonsense and used for sample text was because it wouldn’t form any recognizable words, allowing viewers to focus more on the way that the text looks rather than what it means. Something tells me Cicero wouldn’t really appreciate this misuse of his work.
